Pupusas

Description:

This Salvadoran staple can be made with extremely simple ingredients. Hell, you could be in an active warzone and make this!

Ingredients:

  • corn flour (Maseca is preferred)
  • cheese (traditionally a Salvadoran melting cheese but Oaxacan or Mozzerella works in a pinch)
  • water
  • salt

Steps:

First, you're going to make a kind of dough out of a magical balance of corn flour, water and a little salt. Not too sticky and not too dry is the key. This step is the most important (and difficult) part!

Once the dough is nice and pliable, take a small ball and flatten it in the palm of your hand into a disc

Next, add the cheese (not too much) into the middle of your disc. Optionally, you could add refried beans, pork, etc. Go crazy!

Wrap the disc into a small ball, make sure all the ingredients are nice and covered inside.

With the proto-pupusa ball in hand, start to massage it into a disc-like shape. Be careful not to let it rip!

Last step! Heat up a pan and add a tiny bit of oil before tossing your pupusa down. Flip as needed. Once it's nice and browned (with a couple of burnt cheese spots), it's done. Serve with curtido.

If you're a real guerilla fighter (or just my boomer aunt) you flip piping hot pupusas by hand!
